Guardiola praises Bernardo Silva after Manchester City's win over Arsenal Guardiola praises Bernardo Silva after Manchester City's win over Arsenal Hesport - DPA Monday, April 20, 2026 - 10:30 Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola stated that Bernardo Silva's performance against Arsenal perfectly encapsulated his exceptional career with the Sky Blues. Bernardo Silva shone once again in City's 2-1 victory over Arsenal on Sunday, in the highlight match of the 33rd round of the English Premier League. The most notable moment of his leadership was his lightning-fast run against Arsenal's Kai Havertz, which culminated in a successful clearance in the dying moments of the thrilling encounter. Manchester City announced last Thursday that the Portuguese star would be leaving the club at the end of the season. When Guardiola was asked how he felt about the number 20 after today's presentation, he replied simply: "Gratitude. If I talk too much, I'll cry." Guardiola added: "I want to say a huge thank you to him on behalf of this club for everything he has given. Bernardo has proven that football starts here (pointing to his head) and goes all the way to his feet. This guy isn't the fastest, but he knows exactly what is required at every moment." Guardiola explained in moving words: "He is always committed and never gets injured. Last season was the perfect example of that. While others were absent, he was always there, fighting and suffering as if it were his first day with the team. His mentality is amazing; he always sees the positive in life, and when you have that spirit, you deserve the highest praise." The Spanish coach stressed: “When you write the word (legend), you should write it in capital letters, not only because of what he did today, but because of every match he played throughout his nine years here. These years we spent together would not have been so special without him. He is a truly special player, and any team he moves to will be very lucky to have him.”
